Let's Explore Neptune!

Neptune's Number

The Eighth Planet

Neptune is the eighth planet in our solar system and furthest gas planet from the sun.


Neptune is further from the sun than Uranus and closer than Pluto.

Color

Blue

Neptune is a gas planet with a deep blue color.


Its color helps explain why it was named after the Roman god of the sea.

Orbit

Not Like the Others

Neptune's orbit is tilted compared to the rest of the planet's orbits.


This causes Neptune to be further form the sun than Pluto from time to time.

Surface

A Gas Planet

Being a gas planet there is no real surface to Neptune.


Yet the gravity on Neptune's surface is almost the same as on Earth's surface.


Neptune has the second strongest gravity pull in the solar system.

The Great Dark Spot

Seems Familiar

Just like Jupiter Neptune has a large spot visible on its surface.


The Great Dark Spot is a large storm on Neptune's surface gas.


Strangely this spot disappears at certain times.

Time

Years and Days

A day on Neptune is 19 hours on Earth.


Yet a year for Neptune is 164.8 years on Earth.


This means that Neptune spins faster than Earth but orbits the sun slower.
