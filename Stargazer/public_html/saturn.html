Let's Explore Saturn!

Saturn's Number

The Sixth Planet

Saturn is the sixth planet in our solar system and second gas planet from the sun.


Saturn is further from the sun than Jupiter and closer than Uranus.

Color

Yellow

Saturn is a pale yellow color.


Jupiter may have brighter colors but Saturn is impressive in other ways.

Rings

The Rings

Saturn's rings are made of chunks of rock and ice orbiting around the planet.


While other planets have rings Saturn's are the brightest.

Moons

There's a Lot

Saturn has more than 50 moons, some of which are in its rings.


The gravity pull of these moons help keep the rings in place.

Size

And Shape

Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system.


Strangely though Saturn is wider than it is tall.

  • 6th planet from the sun
  • 2nd largest planet
  • pale yellow color
  • Saturn is wider than it is tall (diameter > distance from pole to pole)
  • Saturn has the most prominent rings
    • -There are three visible rings (ring A and B are the more visible ones)
    • -The rings are composed of particles (like rocks and ice, mostly ice)
    • -These particles can range from a centimeter to several meters
  • Saturn's moons help keep the rings in place
  • 1,429,400,000 km (9.54 AU) from Sun (aka 888,187,982 miles)
  • 74,897 mile diameter
  • Saturn is the least dense planet (less dense than water)
  • 75% hydrogen and 25% helium.
  • 12,000 k at the core
  • Saturn also has bands in its clouds (though much fainter than Jupiter's)
  • Saturn has long lived (though temporary) oval clouds (like the big red spot)
  • The rings aren't stable, but are fed by breaking satellites
