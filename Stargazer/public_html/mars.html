Let's Explore Mars!

Color

It's red

Mars is the only red solid planet in our system. What causes its color?


The dirt and rocks on Mars are rich in iron and the color is caused by rust in this iron.

Size

How big is it?

Mars is slightly larger than half the size of the Earth.


This makes it the third smallest planet in this system being larger than Mercury and Pluto.

Mar's Number

Position in the Solar System

Mars is the fourth planet in the solar system.


This means it orbits further from the sun than Earth but closer than Jupiter.

Orbit

Elliptical

Mars' orbit around the sun looks like a flattened circle.

Because of this Mars gets closer and further from the sun throughout its orbit.

Surface

No Plate Tectonics

Unlike the Earth, Mars' surface doesn't have chunks of land moving around.

Mars' surface remains relatively the same unless objects like meteors hit it, leaving craters.

Ice Caps

Northern versus Southern

Mars, like Earth, has a northern and southern ice cap, but they aren't the same.

The northern ice cap is made up of ice made of water but the southern ice cap is dry ice.

Atmosphere

Dust Storms

Due to Mars' atmosphere being thin strong winds can cause dust storms.

These storms can last for a very long time and rage over Mars' entire surface.

Temperature

It gets cold.

Temperature on Mars' surface can range from 80 F to around 200 F below.

At its warmest Mars would feel like an average summer day.

At its coldest you would be a popsicle.
