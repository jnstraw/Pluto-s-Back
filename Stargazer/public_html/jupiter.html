Let's Explore Jupiter!

  • Fifth planet from the sun
  • Largest planet in solar system
  • 4th brightest object in the sky (after the sun, moon, and Venus)
  • (Jupiter's Bands) Light bands are called zones and the dark bands are belts.
  • (Great Red Spot) Big enough to hold two Earths
  • Jupiter has 4 large moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto)
  • -Jupiter is a gas planet and doesn't have a solid surface
  • Mass of Jupiter (1.900e27 kg) = 318 times Earth's mass
  • -Orbits 483,631,840 miles from the sun
  • -Diameter = 88846 miles
  • -90% hydrogen and 10% helium
  • -Jupiter probably has a core of rocky material amounting to something like 10 to 15 Earth-masses.
  • -Jupiter's magnetic field results from liquid hydrogen towards the center.
  • -Jupiter's bands:Caused by winds that follow the planet's latitude,-Adjacent bands blow in opposing directions,-These winds are driven by Jupiter's internal heat rather than solar heat.
  • -The Great Red Spot:Is a large storm visible on Jupiter's surface,-An oval about 12,000 by 25,000 km,-Discovered more than 300 years ago
  • -Jupiter's core is likely about 20,000k (that's kelvin) due to gravitational compression of theplanet's
  • -Jupiter is as big as can be. Gravity would immerse new material into Jupiter's mass.
  • -Jupiter's magnetic field extends past the orbit of Saturn (650 million km).
  • -Jupiter has dark faint rings
  • -Jupiter can be seen in the night sky
  • -Jupiter's moons are slowly drifting away.
