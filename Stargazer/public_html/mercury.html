Let's Explore Mercury!

What's Mercury's number?

Mercury's position

Mercury is the 1st planet from the sun.

Any moons?

Nope!

Mercury is one of the two planets without a moon.


Can you find the other one?

Size

It's the smallest!

Mercury is the smallest sized planet in our Solar System.

Density

2nd Place

Mercury is the second densest planet in our Solar System.

Speedy Mercury

What a year!

One year on Mercury is 88 days on Earth.


This means it only takes 88 days to circle the Sun, faster than any other planet.

Discovered by...

No one knows!

Unlike other planets, no one knows who was the first to discover Mercury.

Under the Surface

The Core

Recent studies show that Mercury might have a molten core inside of it, much like the Earth does.

A Bumpy Ride

Craters

Mercury has more craters than any other planet in our Solar System.

Second Hottest

Not so Hot

Even though it is the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury is only the second hottest planet.


However, it's still too hot to sustain life.

Mercury's Name

What's in a name?

Mercury was named after the Roman messenger to the Gods.

Temperature Change

Hot and Cold

Mercury has the most extreme temperature change in the entire Solar System.

Asteroid Impact

Long ago...

Scientist believe Mercury survived an impact with a giant asteroid many many years ago.

A Day on Mercury

Longer than a year?

Because it moves around the Sun so quickly, a day on Mercury lasts longer than a year!

Ice

Cool as Ice

Despite being so close to the Sun, Mercury has ice along parts of its surface.

Mercury's Tails

Mercury's Tails

Mercury has streams of particles that come off of its surface to creatye "tails".

Volcanoes

Long ago...

Scientist belive that long ago Mercury used to have volcanoes on its surface.

How Close?

Close to the Sun

Mercury is only 57,909,175 km, or 0.38 AU, away from the Sun (35,983,095 miles).

From the Earth?

Distance from the Earth

Mercury is 77,000,000 km (48,000,000 miles) away from Earth.

Hubble Space Telescope

Can't see Mercury

Because it's so close to the Sun, Mecury can't be seen by the Hubble Sapce Telescope.

Without a Telescope

You can see Mercury

Mercury can be seen without the use of a telescope.

Smaller than Moons

How small?

There are a number of moons in the Solar System that are larger than Mecury.
