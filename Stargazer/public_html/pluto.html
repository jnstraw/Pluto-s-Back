Let's Explore Pluto!

Some facts about mars:

  • Pluto is a dwarf planet
  • Pluto is smaller than seven moons in the solar system (the Moon, Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto, Titan and Triton).
  • Pluto is the ninth and last planet(?)
  • Pluto orbits 5,913,520,000 km (39.5 AU) from the Sun (average)
  • Pluto's diameter is 2372 km
  • Pluto's orbit is usually beyond Neptune's orbit
  • Pluto's orbit crosses closer to the sun than Neptune at times.
  • Pluto has 5 moons: Charon, Hydra, Nix, Kerberos, Styx
  • Pluto is about twice the size of Charon
  • Pluto and Charon orbit about a point between the two (Charon doesn't orbit about Pluto)
  • Pluto's atmosphere is mostly nitrogen, but also has some methane.
  • Pluto's atmosphere is separated into two layers of haze.
  • Pluto has a heart shaped glacier region called Sputnik Planum (made of nitrogen, carbon monoxide and methane ices).
  • Pluto was classified as a "dwarf planet" in 2006.
  • Pluto rotates in opposite to most planets.
  • Pluto's equator is almost perpendicular to its plane of orbit.
  • The temp of pluto's surface is -235 to -210 degrees celsius.
